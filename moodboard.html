03 — Design Principles
PRINCIPLE 01

Specimen, Not Exhibit

Each artwork is treated as a recovered artifact under study — not a display piece. The mounting system is the research apparatus. Labels are field notes. The gallery is the lab.

PRINCIPLE 02

Uniform Container, Unique Contents

The acrylic carriers provide visual consistency — same materials, same grid, same labeling system. But what's mounted on them is wildly diverse. The contrast is the point.

PRINCIPLE 03

Living Data

OLED screens on each panel show real-time Maestra network activity. The specimens aren't static — they're communicating. The archive is alive and we're watching it happen.

04 — Panel Assembly System View Laser Cut Files →

Backplate — Smoked Acrylic

3mm tinted acrylic (dark gray or amber). Laser-etched alignment grid, topographic contours, and specimen catalog ID. The "research document" that everything sits on.

Mounting Layer — Clear/Frosted

3mm clear or frosted acrylic with custom cutouts per artist's devices. Brass hex standoffs create 15-20mm air gap for cooling and cable routing. Devices float above the grid.

Info Strip — Black Acrylic

Specimen catalog entry with OLED screen showing live Maestra network data. Edge-lit with fluorescent acrylic accent strip — each artist gets a unique glow color.

Cable Management

Braided mesh sleeving in matte black. Heat-shrink labels at each terminus in matching monospace font. Cables route between layers through dedicated slots.

Optional: Thermal Unit

40mm Noctua fan bracket for heat-generating devices. Mounts below panel. The layer gap creates a natural convection chimney — fan assists when needed.
